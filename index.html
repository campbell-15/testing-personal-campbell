About Us

Pioneering sustainable solutions for a better future

B10 is a producer of biochar. Our initial focus will be in South Africa, although we believe that our business can expand into other countries in due course. We are investing in the latest technology. Our biochar will be produced in advanced fully-automated continuous kilns from waste biomass materials that would otherwise be simply burned or allowed to rot.

Carbon Locking

The B10 Way

Our unique approach to carbon storage

The oldest known charcoal from a fireplace was discovered in Wonderwerk Cave in South Africa, dating back approximately 1 million years. This ancient charcoal serves as a practical example of the longevity of charcoal, having locked up carbon for a million years. Biochar, a very pure form of charcoal, has a carbon content of up to 90% by mass.

Trees are an integral part of the natural carbon cycle. Through photosynthesis, they sequester carbon from atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) and release oxygen. When biomass is burned or naturally decays, the carbon is returned to the atmosphere as CO2, continuing the cycle.

Creating biochar from wood interrupts this natural carbon cycle. The carbon content of the wood is converted into a very stable form of carbon through a high-heat, low-oxygen pyrolysis process. This process produces a substance that remains stable for thousands of years, and as demonstrated, up to a million years.

What B10 does with biochar can be described as "reverse coal mining." Instead of extracting carbon (coal) that was sequestered millions of years ago and burning it, thus adding CO2 to the atmosphere, we reverse the process. We produce (char)coal and return it to the earth for long-term carbon storage.

Social Impact

Building stronger communities through sustainable practices

At B10, not only do we want to make a positive difference to our environment, but also have a positive impact on the communities surrounding our projects. In South Africa, unemployment and lack of job opportunities is a major issue effecting the poorer sections of our society.

Environmental Impact

Leading the fight against climate change

CO2 Reduction

The world is racing to limit its carbon emissions so that the growth in global temperatures can be halted. We need not only to reduce emissions, but to actively take carbon out of the atmosphere. This is known as CDR, or Carbon Dioxide Removal.

The best machines to take CO2 out of the atmosphere are trees. Using renewable biomass for biochar has the potential to be the single biggest driver of CDR. The trees take the carbon out of the atmosphere, and biochar prevents it ever going back into the atmosphere.

With our top-of-the-range machines, we perform a tried and tested methodology, and it is highly scalable. Biochar production preserves the carbon dioxide captured by the trees and also improves local air quality as the biomass no longer needs to be burned.
